This is a note to self

Many conversations we have, are fairly repetitive. Conversations like:

  1. “How was your XXX vacation?”
  2. “Hey, I joined the company/team recently”
  3. Any kind of introduction conversations

It's a good idea to mentally create scripts for these conversation beforehand.

You will form a script anyways after the first 3-4 conversations. The trick is to do it in advanced so that even those first 3-4 conversations are good. 

If you are joining a new company and introducing yourself to a team members, your goal is to get to a good, functional, working relationship as soon as possible. So the script should include:

  * Telling your background as quickly as possible. You don’t need to boast about yourself. You aren’t trying to impress this person. Just get them to like you.
  * Understanding what they do at the company
  * Getting their opinion on the product/ what can be done better etc. Some gossip to build rapport
  * Maybe tell a joke or two to create a rapport
  * Understand what they expect from you in future

The script for a tale of the vacation might include

  * 2-3 novel things you experienced
  * A story about something that happened to you. Preferably something funny. 
  * A piece of advice if they want to travel at that destination.

Create a script. Be pro at conversations. Thats it.
